Eggnog

Eggnog is a rich and creamy holiday beverage that is enjoyed by many during the festive season. It is made with eggs, milk, cream, sugar, and a combination of warm spices like nutmeg, cinnamon, and cloves.

Preparation Time

30 mins

Cook Time

2-3 hours

Servings

6-8 servings

Instructions

Step 1

Bring milk to a simmer. While the milk is simmering, have the egg yolks and sugar ready in a separate bowl and using an electric mixer, whip them until they are light in color.

Step 2

Once the milk has simmered, slowly temper the milk into the egg yolk mixture by gradually scooping in a little at a time until milk has fully combined with the egg yolk mixture. Think about the process of making pastry cream, they are practically identical.

Step 3

Add vanilla bean.

Step 4

Consistently stir for 10-15 minutes using a spatula in low heat to prevent curdling. Do not boil. Look for a nappe consistency (coats the back of a spoon when you run your finger through it.)

Step 5

Strain through a fine mesh sieve in a metal bowl.

Step 6

Place on ice water bath.

Step 7

Add cinnamon, clove, ginger powder, nutmeg, rum or brandy, and heavy cream.

Step 8

Store and serve as needed.

Step 9

To finish, add extra whipped egg whites, alcohol, or more nutmeg as desired in a glass.

Ingredients

3 cups whole milk

1/2 vanilla bean or 1.5 teaspoon vanilla extract

6 egg yolks

1 cup granulated sugar

1tsp cinnamon

Heaping 1/4 tsp cloves

1tbsp plus 2tsp ginger powder

1.5tsp ground nutmeg

1.5 cups heavy cream
