English Muffins

One of the most famous fast-food breakfasts in the world could be so much more... and we are going to make it just that. Homemade English muffins, perfectly cooked eggs, crispy homemade pan sausage, cheddar, and light spicy mayo. Let's see how we do.

Preparation Time

30 mins

Cook Time

30 mins

Servings

2 servings

Instructions

Step 1

Start by whisking together warm whole milk (95F) with instant yeast and granulated sugar until dissolved. Let sit at room temperature for 5 minutes.

Step 2

In the bowl of a stand mixer, add bread flour and fine sea salt. Mix together using the dough hook attachment until incorporated.

Step 3

Add in sugar yeast milk mixture along with lightly melted butter, and an egg. Mix until dough forms.

Step 4

Shape the dough into a tight ball and place into a large greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap and let rise at room temperature for 1 hour or until doubled.

Step 5

Punch the air out of the dough and divide it into 15 pieces. Shape into balls and place in a dough proof box, or rimmed baking sheet. Dust with cornmeal and cover, resting for 10 minutes.

Step 6

Take a dough ball, dust with cornmeal, and flatten. Place in a cast-iron skillet over medium-low heat cooking for 3-5 minutes per side.

Step 7

Add to a rimmed baking sheet and bake in a preheated oven at 350F for 7-10 minutes. Let cool on a wire rack.

Step 8

In a medium-size bowl, combine pork, sage, thyme, fennel, msg, salt, cayenne, brown sugar, sea salt, and nutmeg. Grind that through your meat grinder once until all of it is ground and then one more time to get it emulsified and fine; finally, knead that lightly and reserve.

Step 9

In a lightly greased hot pan over medium-high, roll your meat into balls (around 4 ounces), place it in the middle of your pan, then press it with a spatula or a burger press until about a 1/4" thick and sear for one to two minutes. Flip and sear the other side for another minute or until fully cooked.

Step 10

Add the mayo, creme fraiche, sriracha, smoked paprika, garlic powder, Worcestershire sauces salt and pepper to taste. Mix until fully incorporated.

Step 11

In a nonstick skillet over medium heat, add and melt butter. Once butter is bubbling, add a lightly greased ring mold in the center of the pan.

Step 12

Carefully crack the egg into the ring mold. Season the top with salt and cook for 30 seconds. Add water around the mold, and immediately cover with foil and let it steam for 45 seconds. Remove the egg from the pan.

Ingredients

2 cups whole milk

3.5 tbsp granulated sugar

2.5 tsp instant yeast

1 egg

1/4 cup unsalted butter, lightly melted

4 1/3 cup bread flour

2 tsp fine sea salt

Cornmeal for dusting

2 lbs ground pork

1 tablespoon fresh sage leaves, finely chopped

1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves, finely chopped

1.5 teaspoons fennel powder

1/2 teaspoon cayenne

Pinch of fresh nutmeg

1 tablespoon brown sugar

2.5 teaspoons fine sea salt

1/2 teaspoon MSG

Egg

Salt and pepper to taste

Butter

1/2 cup mayo

1/4 cup creme fraiche

2.5 tbsp sriracha

1 tbsp smoked paprika

1 tbsp garlic powder

2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
