Nasi Lemak

Malaysian coconut milk rice, served with sambal, fried crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts and cucumber. This is the best and most authentic nasi lemak recipe.

Preparation Time

30 mins

Cook Time

30 mins

Servings

2 servings

Instructions

Step 1

Just like making steamed rice, rinse your rice and drain. Add the coconut milk, a pinch of salt, and some water. Add the pandan leaves into the rice and cook your rice.

Step 2

Rinse the dried anchovies and drain the water. Fry the anchovies until they turn light brown and put them aside.

Step 3

Lightly toast the peanuts in a frying pan until golden brown.

Step 4

Pound the prawn paste together with shallots, garlic, and deseeded dried chilies with a mortar and pestle. You can also grind them with a food processor. Slice the red onion into rings.

Step 5

Soak the tamarind pulp in water for 15 minutes. Squeeze the tamarind constantly to extract the flavor into the water. Drain the pulp and save the tamarind juice.

Step 6

Heat some oil in a pan and fry the spice paste until fragrant. Add in the onion rings. Add in the dried anchoivies and stir well. Add tamarind juice, salt, and sugar. Simmer on low heat until the gravy thickens. Set aside.

Ingredients

2 cups rice

3 screwpine leaves tie them into a knot

Salt to taste

150 ml-180 ml coconut milk

Water

Tamarind pulp

1/2 red onion

1 cup dried anchovies

1 clove garlic

4 shallots

10 dried chillies

1 teaspoon prawn paste

1 tablespoon sugar

2 hard boiled eggs

1 small cucumber cut into slices

2 cups of peanut
