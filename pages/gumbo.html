Gumbo

Homemade Gumbo should be a cooks right of passage. And fine, maybe this gumbo isn't exactly perfect, but it's flavor profile is pretty dang perfect when you get that first spoonful.

Preparation Time

30 mins

Cook Time

30 mins

Servings

2 servings

Instructions

Step 1

De-shell the lobster by cutting the hard ribs on the underside, giving it a gentle crack on its side, carefully opening it up, fiddling the meat from its shell, and reserving the flesh and the shell separately.

Step 2

Break down three snow crab clusters, reserve the meat, and place the shells with the rest of the stock ingredients.

Step 3

In a big pot over medium-high, add enough oil to coat the bottom, add the bones, the chuck roast, sear the beef on all sides, for two to three minutes or until caramelized.

Step 4

Add in all the shellfish shells, along with the vegetables, black peppercorns, and bay leaves. Next, add the water, bring it to a boil, reduce to low heat, and simmer, skimming off any scum that rises to the top, and let it cook for five to six hours.

Step 5

Remove the solids, strain through a fine-mesh sieve. Reserve.

Step 6

In a large stockpot (10 quarts), add the duck fat, pork fat, heat that over medium heat; once it’s melted, add the flour, mix it, and let it cook, occasionally stirring until a dark mahogany color, around 15 minutes.

Step 7

Cut both sausages on a half an inch piece on a bias. Reserve.

Step 8

Add the celery, onion, and bell pepper to the pot, sauté until soft, add the garlic, sauté until fragrant, then add the sausages and let it rend some of the fat.

Step 9

Add the Cajun seasoning, let it toast for one minute, then add the Louisiana hot sauce, beef stock, tomatoes, and bring it to a boil, lower the heat to medium-low, and simmer for one hour.

Step 10

Add the filet powder (first measurement), simmer for 25 minutes, add the bottom segment from two snow crab clusters, remove the meat from the rest of the clusters, and place it to the side.

Step 11

Add three whole snow crab clusters, broken into individual pieces, then add the shrimp, the devein lobster (one-inch chunks). Lower the heat to low, simmer for 15 minutes.

Step 12

Add file powder (second measurement), and simmer for 5 minutes. Then add all of the king crab meat and the Worcestershire sauce. Once the meat is hot, it is ready to serve.

Ingredients

2 onions, quartered

2 carrots, rough chop

1 lb shrimp shells

1 lb crab or lobster shells

2 lbs boneless beef chuck roast

1 tablespoon (9g) black peppercorns

3 bay leaves

5 quarts water

4 thyme sprigs

1/2 cup duck fat

1/2 cup pork fat

1 cup all-purpose flour

3 ribs celery, finely chopped

2 onion, finely chopped

1 large green bell pepper, finely chopped

4 cloves garlic, minced

1/2 lbs andouille

1/2 lb kielbasa

1.5 tablespoons cajun seasoning

3 tablespoons Louisana hot sauce

3.25 quarts stock

1 20oz canned crushed fire-roasted tomatoes

2.5 teaspoons file powder - first measurement

5 king crab legs

2 pounds uncooked shrimp, peeled and deveined

4 lobster tails

3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce

Salt to taste
